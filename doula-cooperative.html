Doula Cooperative

This is the first built-from-scratch, sent to production website that I've built. It utilizes HTML5, CSS3, and Javascript to deliver a recreated website to the organization.

The Doula Cooperative was stuck with a complete hosting solution and website builder (SquareSpace) that was costing them $200 per year. I was able to recreate their existing site and bring it to a self-hosted solution so they could avoid that hefty cost each year.

Recreating sites brings me back to the days when I was a graphic designer at a print shop and customers would bring in old business cards, letterhead, etc. with no digital copy. It was the early 2000s. I would have to scan in the graphic, trace it, and match fonts to try and reproduce the artwork as closely as possible.

Working on this website really gave me confidence to know that the TechDegree from Treehouse really taught me how to build sites. No frameworks were used in this site, and only JQuery was used for the menu functionality.
